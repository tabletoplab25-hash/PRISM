PRISM

A Strategic Color Card Game for 2-6 Players

Overview

PRISM is a tactical card game where each color has unique scoring abilities. Players must carefully choose which cards to play and which to pass to opponents, creating a dynamic game of strategy, prediction, and calculated risks.

Components

  • Unique Deck: 5 colors (Red, Blue, Green, Yellow, Purple)
  • Card distribution varies by player count (see table below)
  • Each player receives exactly 10 cards

Card Values

Cards are divided into 4 point tiers based on their number. Higher numbers are worth more points.

Setup

  1. Shuffle all cards together
  2. Deal 10 cards to each player
  3. All players start with 0 points

Game Flow

The game consists of 10 rounds. Each round follows these phases:

1. Selection Phase

All players simultaneously choose one card from their hand to play face-down.

2. Reveal Phase

All cards are revealed simultaneously. Points are calculated based on each color's special ability (see Color Abilities section).

3. Passing Phase

Each player chooses one card from their remaining hand to pass to the player on their right. This is a strategic decision that affects future rounds.

Color Abilities

The core of PRISM's strategy lies in understanding how each color scores:

üî¥ RED - Double or Nothing

The LOWEST red card scores double points. All other red cards score negative points.

Example: Red 2 (1pt), Red 5 (3pt), Red 7 (4pt) are played.
‚Ä¢ Red 2 (1pt base) = +2 points (doubled!)
‚Ä¢ Red 5 (3pt base) = -3 points
‚Ä¢ Red 7 (4pt base) = -4 points

üîµ BLUE - Shared Pool

All blue card points are added together and divided equally among players who played blue (round down).

Example: Three players play Blue 2 (1pt), Blue 4 (2pt), and Blue 6 (3pt).
‚Ä¢ Total pool: 1 + 2 + 3 = 6 points
‚Ä¢ Each player gets: 6 √∑ 3 = 2 points

üü¢ GREEN - Anti-Majority

Each green card loses 1 point for every LOWER green card played (minimum 0).

Example: Green 2, Green 5, Green 8 are played.
‚Ä¢ Green 2 (1pt) - 0 lower greens = 1 point
‚Ä¢ Green 5 (3pt) - 1 lower green = 2 points
‚Ä¢ Green 8 (4pt) - 2 lower greens = 2 points

üü° YELLOW - Relative Scoring

+1 point for each OTHER yellow card played.
-1 point for each LOWER card of OTHER colors played.

Example: You play Yellow 8 (4pt base). Others play: Yellow 5, Yellow 10, Red 3, Green 6, Blue 7.
‚Ä¢ Base: 4 points
‚Ä¢ +2 points (two other yellows: 5 and 10)
‚Ä¢ -3 points (three lower other colors: Red 3, Green 6, Blue 7)
‚Ä¢ Total: 4 + 2 - 3 = 3 points

Note: Yellow 1 is always safe (no lower cards possible) but scores fewer points. High yellows score more base points but risk many lower cards!

üü£ PURPLE - Cooperation (Player Count Dependent)

2-3 Players: Purple scores positive ONLY if you're the ONLY purple played. If others also play purple, you score negative.

4-6 Players: Purple scores positive if at least 1 OTHER purple is played. If you're the only purple, you score negative.

Example (2 players):
‚Ä¢ If only you play purple ‚Üí Positive!
‚Ä¢ If both play purple ‚Üí Both negative!

Example (5 players):
‚Ä¢ If 2+ purples played ‚Üí All purples positive! (cooperation)
‚Ä¢ If only 1 purple played ‚Üí That player negative (no cooperation)

End of Game

After 10 rounds, the game ends. The player with the highest total score wins!

Strategy Tips

  • Red: Play low to win big, but watch out for even lower reds!
  • Blue: Safe and consistent. Good for medium-value cards.
  • Green: Play high to avoid penalties from lower greens.
  • Yellow: Complex but powerful. Predict what others will play.
  • Purple: Risk everything for cooperation (or uniqueness in 2-3p).
  • Passing: Give away dangerous cards or set up future combinations.
  • Card Counting: Remember what colors have been played to predict opponents' hands.

Game Variants

PRISM offers different variants to adjust the level of information and strategic depth.

Standard Variant (Hidden Information)

This is the classic PRISM experience. All players start with 10 cards in hand, completely hidden from opponents. This creates maximum uncertainty and emphasizes:

  • Memory and card counting
  • Risk assessment
  • Psychological reads
  • Adapting to unexpected reveals

Open Information Variant (Recommended for Strategic Players)

This variant reduces blind play and adds visible planning opportunities.

üé≤ Setup Differences

Each player receives their 10 cards split into:

  • 5 cards in hand (hidden from opponents)
  • 5 visible cards (face-up, visible to all players)

üìã Gameplay Rules

Playing Cards:

  • You can ONLY play cards from your hand (hidden cards)
  • Visible cards cannot be played directly
  • This maintains suspense while giving strategic information

Passing Cards:

  • Priority 1: Visible cards are passed FIRST (automatically, one per round)
  • When you pass a visible card, it becomes HIDDEN for the player who receives it
  • Priority 2: Once all visible cards are exhausted, pass from your hand as in Standard variant
Example Round in Open Variant:

Setup: You have Yellow 8, Blue 3, Red 2 visible. In hand: Purple 5, Green 9, Red 10, Blue 7, Yellow 4.

Round 1 - Play: You play Purple 5 from your hand. Your visible cards remain unchanged.

Round 1 - Pass: Your first visible card (Yellow 8) is automatically passed to the right. That player receives it in their hand (hidden). You now have 4 visible cards and 4 cards in hand.

Strategic Note: Everyone saw you pass Yellow 8! They know your opponent received it and can plan accordingly.

Why Play Open Information?

  • Less "blind" gameplay: You know what cards are coming and where they'll go
  • Enhanced strategy: Plan several rounds ahead based on visible information
  • Social dynamics: "I'm passing you a Purple 10... you'd better play it!"
  • Reduced luck: Skilled players can better predict and control outcomes
  • Teaching tool: New players can observe what cards are in play
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Once visible cards are depleted (after 5 rounds), both variants play identically. This creates an interesting transition from open information to hidden information mid-game!
